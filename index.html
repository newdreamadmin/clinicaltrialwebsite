Advancing Clinical Trials Through Data and Intelligence

We are dedicated to improving how clinical trials are conducted by combining medical research expertise with modern technology. Our goal is to help healthcare organizations identify eligible patients faster, streamline trial operations, and accelerate the development of life-saving treatments.

Clinical trials play a critical role in advancing medicine, yet patient recruitment and eligibility screening remain major challenges. Despite the widespread adoption of Electronic Health Records (EHR), identifying suitable patients for clinical trials often requires time-consuming manual review of complex medical data.

Our platform leverages Artificial Intelligence to analyze structured and unstructured clinical data from EHR systems, enabling automated patient identification, improved recruitment efficiency, and better clinical trial management. By bridging the gap between healthcare data and clinical research, we help researchers conduct trials more efficiently and bring innovative therapies to patients faster.

Dr Varun Sundaram, MBBS, PhD, MSc, FRCP (UK)

Dr Varun Sundaram is an Associate Professor of Medicine at Case Western Reserve University School of Medicine and serves as Section Chief of Advanced Heart Failure at the Louis Stokes Cleveland Veterans Affairs Medical Center. He is also Deputy Director of the Health Services Research Center and a Critical Care Cardiologist at University Hospital Cleveland Medical Center.

Dr Sundaram holds a PhD in Epidemiology from Imperial College London and an MSc in Clinical Trials from the University of Oxford. His research focuses on epidemiology and clinical trials in heart failure, and he has received multiple nationally competitive grants from organizations such as the National Institutes of Health (NIH), the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, and the American Heart Association.

With extensive experience in clinical trial design and execution, Dr Sundaram leads the scientific and clinical research vision of Clinical Trial AI.

Baskaran Selvaraj

Baskaran Selvaraj is a Founder and Technology Leader at Newdream Data Systems with extensive expertise in enterprise data platforms, large-scale system integration, and modern application development.

He has led the design and implementation of scalable and secure technology infrastructures that support complex data-driven applications across industries. His work focuses on building reliable platforms capable of processing large volumes of structured and unstructured data.

At Clinical Trial AI, Baskaran leads the platform architecture and engineering strategy, ensuring that the system integrates effectively with healthcare data sources such as Electronic Health Records while maintaining scalability, security, and operational efficiency.

Dr Chloe Bloom, MBChB, MSc, PhD

Dr Chloe Bloom is an Associate Professor in Respiratory Epidemiology at Imperial College London and an Honorary Respiratory Consultant. She specializes in the use of large-scale electronic healthcare datasets to improve respiratory outcomes and clinical research methodologies.

Dr Bloom has authored more than 70 publications in leading medical and respiratory journals, with research that has influenced clinical guidelines and national healthcare policy. Her work focuses on generating real-world evidence and designing pragmatic studies that translate healthcare data into actionable clinical insights.

Within Clinical Trial AI, Dr Bloom contributes expertise in epidemiology, healthcare data analysis, and real-world evidence generation to support intelligent patient identification and trial-ready insights.

Professor Sir Stephen Bloom

Professor Sir Stephen Bloom is a British Professor of Medicine at Imperial College London and an internationally recognized leader in metabolic and obesity research.

His pioneering work on gut hormones laid the scientific foundation for GLP-1–based therapies used today in the treatment of obesity and metabolic disease. In recognition of his contributions to medical science, he was knighted in the Queen’s New Year Honours in 2012 and elected a Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS) in 2013.

Professor Bloom has also founded and successfully exited multiple biotechnology companies based on innovative medical treatments. His experience in translational research and biotech commercialization provides strategic guidance for the scientific direction of Clinical Trial AI.
